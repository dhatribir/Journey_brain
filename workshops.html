Simple 40-minute flow for every week

5 min warm-up 10 min learn 15 min activity 10 min game + reflection

Student note: I wanted this to feel like a small science club, not a school lecture. Kids should click, draw, guess, move, laugh, and leave with one idea they can explain at home.

Week 1

Mission 1: Meet Your Brain

Big idea: Different parts of the brain help with different jobs, but they work together like a team.

01

Where Am I?

Kids click a brain map after hearing situations like “you see a rainbow,” “you hear music,” or “you decide what game to play.”

Skill: Brain partsTime: 10 minSupplies: Website
02

Brain Superhero Factory

Kids draw one lobe as a superhero: Captain Frontal, Vision Hero, Sound Detective, or Sensation Explorer.

Skill: CreativityTime: 10 minSupplies: Paper
03

Brain Relay Game

The leader reads a real-life scene. Kids raise the card for the lobe they think helped first, then explain why.

Skill: Connecting ideasTime: 10 minSupplies: Lobe cards
04

My Brain Today

Kids finish the sentence: “Today my ____ lobe helped me when I ____.” They can draw instead of writing.

Skill: ReflectionTime: 10 minSupplies: Worksheet
Student reflection: While studying for Brain Bee, I was surprised that the brain is not just one “thinking machine.” Every region has a job, and this mission helps younger kids see that with examples from their own lives.
Week 2

Mission 2: Memory Lab

Big idea: Memory gets stronger when we group ideas, connect them to pictures, and review them in fun ways.

01

Mystery Tray Challenge

Show 12–15 objects for 30 seconds, hide them, and let kids write or draw what they remember.

Skill: MemoryTime: 10 minSupplies: Small objects
02

Chunking Magic

Compare remembering a long number like 483927561 with groups like 483 — 927 — 561. Kids feel the difference quickly.

Skill: StrategyTime: 10 minSupplies: Board/cards
03

Build a Memory Palace

Kids draw their home or school and place items in different rooms to remember them in order.

Skill: VisualizationTime: 10 minSupplies: Paper
04

Detective Picture Quiz

Use picture clues instead of only words. Kids answer how the brain can remember better without the hint giving away the answer.

Skill: RecallTime: 10 minSupplies: Website quiz
Student reflection: One topic I enjoyed during Brain Bee preparation was memory. I liked learning that remembering is not magic — there are strategies we can actually practice.
Week 3

Mission 3: Focus, Sleep & Screens

Big idea: The brain learns better when it has focus time, rest, movement, and fewer distractions.

01

Spot-the-Difference Focus Game

Kids compare two pictures and find small changes. Then they repeat with a distraction and compare the result.

Skill: AttentionTime: 10 minSupplies: Images
02

Distraction Experiment

Kids try a short reading or puzzle with background noise, then again quietly. They discuss what changed.

Skill: FocusTime: 10 minSupplies: Timer
03

Sleep Scientist Flow

Kids arrange cards in order: Learn → Sleep → Brain organizes → Memory saved. Keep it visual and simple.

Skill: Cause/effectTime: 10 minSupplies: Cards
04

Brain Recharge Checklist

Kids create a checklist with sleep, water, movement, reading, and screen breaks. They pick one habit to try this week.

Skill: Healthy habitsTime: 10 minSupplies: Worksheet
Student reflection: I wanted this mission to connect neuroscience to daily life. Kids may not remember every term, but they can remember that their brain needs rest and breaks.
Week 4

Mission 4: Music, Emotions & Brain Olympics

Big idea: Music, emotions, movement, and reaction time all show how fast and connected the brain is.

01

Music Detective

Play short music clips. Kids guess the emotion: calm, happy, excited, or tense — then explain what they noticed.

Skill: EmotionTime: 10 minSupplies: Music clips
02

Color Your Feelings

Kids build an emotion color wheel and connect feelings to body signals like heart rate, face, and posture.

Skill: Self-awarenessTime: 10 minSupplies: Markers
03

Reaction Race

Use a ruler-drop test or simple online reaction game. Kids compare reaction times and talk about practice.

Skill: Motor responseTime: 10 minSupplies: Ruler/timer
04

Brain Master Challenge

Final team quiz using pictures from all four missions. End with a Brain Explorer certificate or badge.

Skill: ReviewTime: 10 minSupplies: Quiz
Student reflection: This final mission is meant to feel like a celebration. My goal is for kids to leave thinking, “My brain is doing cool things all the time.”

4 week student-led kids workshop

Brain Explorer Missions

Each week is a 40-minute mission. Kids learn a little, try something hands-on, play a game, and end with a small reflection.

Mission 140 min
🧠

Meet Your Brain

Kids learn the major brain lobes by clicking, acting, drawing, and matching real-life actions to different brain parts.

5 min warm-up10 min brain map15 min create/play10 min reflection
01

Where Am I?

Use the clickable brain map. Kids hear examples like seeing a rainbow or hearing music and pick the brain area.

02

Brain Superhero Factory

Draw one lobe as a superhero: Captain Frontal, Vision Hero, Sound Detective, or Sensation Explorer.

03

Brain Relay Game

The leader reads a real-life scene. Kids hold up the lobe card and explain why they chose it.

04

My Brain Today

Kids finish: “Today my ____ lobe helped me when I ____.” Drawing is allowed.

Supplies

  • Website brain map
  • Paper and markers
  • Simple lobe cards
  • Exit reflection sheet

Student reflection

While studying for Brain Bee, I was surprised that the brain is not just one thinking machine. Every region has a job, and this mission explains that with examples from daily life.

Mission 240 min
📚

Memory Lab

Kids become memory detectives and test how grouping, pictures, places, and practice help the brain remember.

5 min story10 min tray test15 min memory tricks10 min picture quiz
01

Mystery Tray Challenge

Show 12–15 objects for 30 seconds, hide them, and let kids write or draw what they remember.

02

Chunking Magic

Compare a long number with groups like 483 — 927 — 561. Kids feel the difference quickly.

03

Memory Palace Drawing

Kids draw a house or school and place objects in different rooms to remember them in order.

04

Detective Picture Quiz

Use picture clues instead of word-only questions. Hints guide thinking but do not give the answer.

Supplies

  • 12–15 small objects
  • Paper and pencil
  • Number cards
  • Quiz page

Student reflection

One topic I enjoyed during Brain Bee preparation was memory. I liked learning that remembering is not magic — there are strategies we can practice.

Mission 340 min
😴

Focus, Sleep & Screens

Kids test focus and learn why sleep, breaks, water, movement, and fewer distractions help the brain learn.

5 min check-in10 min focus game15 min experiment10 min habit plan
01

Spot-the-Difference Focus Game

Kids compare two pictures and find small changes, then repeat with a distraction.

02

Distraction Experiment

Try a short puzzle with background noise, then again quietly. Compare how the brain felt.

03

Sleep Scientist Flow

Arrange cards: Learn → Sleep → Brain organizes → Memory saved. Keep it visual and simple.

04

Brain Recharge Checklist

Kids create a checklist with sleep, water, movement, reading, and screen breaks.

Supplies

  • Spot-the-difference sheet
  • Timer
  • Sleep flow cards
  • Checklist worksheet

Student reflection

I wanted this mission to connect neuroscience to daily life. Kids may not remember every term, but they can remember that their brain needs rest and breaks.

Mission 440 min
🎵

Music, Emotions & Brain Olympics

Kids explore feelings, sound, reaction time, and finish with a fun Brain Explorer challenge.

5 min warm-up10 min music lab15 min brain games10 min final quiz
01

Music Detective

Play short music clips. Kids guess the emotion: calm, happy, excited, or tense.

02

Color Your Feelings

Kids build an emotion color wheel and connect feelings to body signals.

03

Reaction Race

Use a ruler-drop test or simple reaction game. Kids compare reaction times.

04

Brain Master Challenge

Final team quiz using pictures from all four missions. End with a Brain Explorer badge.

Supplies

  • Short music clips
  • Markers
  • Ruler or timer
  • Final quiz

Student reflection

This final mission is meant to feel like a celebration. My goal is for kids to leave thinking, “My brain is doing cool things all the time.”

Workshop style

Quick story → hands-on challenge → game → reflection. No long lecture.
